Title: 89(2) HR 118 - Enrolled version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Enrolled Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 118 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 118 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the sorrow felt by the family and friends of Kellyanne Elizabeth Lytal of San Antonio, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County; and WHEREAS, The daughter of Malorie and James Lytal, Kellyanne Lytal was born in Houston on May 25, 2017, and she was eight years old at the time of her passing; she shared a strong bond with her younger sister, Emmalynn, and she benefited from the love and support of an extended family that included her grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, and godparents; and WHEREAS, Kellyanne was a student at Cambridge Elementary School, where she excelled academically and was recognized with the Cambridge PTO Excellence Award; her charismatic personality and her welcoming nature made an impression on her teachers and classmates, and she demonstrated her kindness by helping others; she dreamed of becoming an Alamo Heights High School cheerleader, and she hoped to one day attend The University of Texas at Austin and join Kappa Kappa Gamma Sorority; and WHEREAS, Outside of school, Kellyanne was involved in Girl Scouts, and she enjoyed such activities as roller blading, playing softball and soccer, and participating in gymnastics; she also had a passion for singing and dancing, sewing, and fashion, and she spent many happy hours shopping with her mother, playing sports with her father, and being silly with her sister; moreover, Kellyanne's faith played an important role in her life, and she took pleasure in reading Bible stories and attending Sunday school; and WHEREAS, Although her time on this earth was far too brief, Kellyanne Lytal was a ray of light in the lives of those who held her dear, and they will never forget the joy, laughter, and love that she shared with them; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Kellyanne Elizabeth Lytal and extend sincere condolences to the members of her family: to her parents, Malorie Liljenwall Lytal and James Wade Lytal; to her sister, Emmalynn Grace Lytal; to her grandparents, Laurie and Paul Tyler, Mary Beth and James Lytal, and Becky Liljenwall; to her godparents, Lauren and Landon Ludwick; and to her other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Kellyanne Lytal. DeAyala ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 118 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 2, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 118 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 118 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the sorrow felt by the family and friends of Kellyanne Elizabeth Lytal of San Antonio, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County;
