Title: 89(2) HR 160 - Enrolled version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Enrolled Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 160 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 160 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, The citizens of Edinburg lost an esteemed community leader with the passing of Sabrina Walker-Hernandez on July 12, 2025, at the age of 54; and WHEREAS, The daughter of Curtis and Joyce Walker, the former Sabrina Walker was born on January 31, 1971, and she grew up with the companionship of three siblings, Yakesha, Kimberly, and Curtis; answering her nation's call to duty, she served in the U.S. Army for four years; she earned her bachelor's degree in political science and master's degree in public administration from The University of Texas-Pan American, and she also held a nonprofit management certification from Harvard University; and WHEREAS, From 1999 to 2019, Mrs. Walker-Hernandez served as the CEO of the Boys & Girls Clubs of Edinburg Rio Grande Valley; she went on to establish Building Better Boards, where she mentored thousands of nonprofit leaders across the nation; as an inspirational speaker, she presented leadership workshops, keynote speeches, and webinars; and WHEREAS, Mrs. Walker-Hernandez further benefited her community as a board member of such organizations as the Museum of South Texas History, the Edinburg Economic Development Corporation, and the Rotary Club of Edinburg, and she also chaired the Mayor's Youth Advisory Council; in addition, she used her battle with cancer to help others, publicly sharing her journey through an op-ed and raising funds for the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society; in recognition of her efforts, she received a Jim Wetherington Southwesterner Award from the Boys & Girls Clubs of America, and in 2023, she was named the Woman of the Year by the Edinburg Chamber of Commerce; and WHEREAS, In all her endeavors, Mrs. Walker-Hernandez enjoyed the love and support of her husband, Hector Hernandez; she was a devoted mother to her four children, Joseph, Nadia, Destiny, and Amaya, and over the years, she became a proud "Bibi" to five grandchildren, Liam, Austin, Serenity, Avery, and Uriah; and WHEREAS, The loss of Sabrina Walker-Hernandez is keenly felt by the numerous friends that she made through the years as well as by the many people in Texas and beyond whose lives she forever enriched; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Sabrina Walker-Hernandez and extend deepest condolences to all who mourn her passing; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Sabrina Walker-Hernandez. Canales ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 160 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 3, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 160 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 160 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, The citizens of Edinburg lost an esteemed community leader with the passing of Sabrina Walker-Hernandez on July 12, 2025, at the age of 54;
