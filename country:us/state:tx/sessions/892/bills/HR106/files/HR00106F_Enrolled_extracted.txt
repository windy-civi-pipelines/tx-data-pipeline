89(2) HR 106 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 106 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A full and generous life drew to a close with the passing of Linda Joyce Cox on July 21, 2025, at the age of 74; and WHEREAS, Affectionately known as "Tilt," the former Linda Murphree was born to Dale Allen and Jimmy L.  Murphree in Jonesboro, Arkansas, on November 9, 1950, and she grew up with the companionship of two siblings, Nancy and Jim; and WHEREAS, In all her endeavors, she benefited from the love and support of her husband, Lucky, and she took great pride in her children, Nick, Dawnn, and Dillon; over the years, she had the pleasure of welcoming into her family six grandchildren; and WHEREAS, A passionate community advocate, Mrs. Cox made a meaningful difference in the lives of countless children, families, and other individuals in need through her contributions to Tilt's Toy Run, Wishing on the Lone Star, and the Special Needs Prom; she also organized a number of other local events, and she helped collect and distribute donations for people experiencing homelessness; and WHEREAS, Mrs. Cox enjoyed traveling the open road on her Harley-Davidson motorcycle, and she shared lifelong bonds with a close-knit community of bikers; in addition, she operated a booth at the Texas State Fair; and WHEREAS, Admired for her creativity, kindness, and generosity, Linda Cox brightened the world for all who knew her, and she will forever hold a special place in their hearts; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life of Linda Joyce Cox and extend sincere condolences to the members of her family: to her husband, Lucky Cox; to her children, Nick and his partner, Kim, Dawnn, and Dillon; to her sister, Nancy; to her nieces, Jennifer, Michelle, Rachael, and Heather; to her grandchildren, Haygen, Leeland, Whitten, Aubriella, Leo, and Joann; and to her other relatives and many friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Linda Cox. Bowers ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 106 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 2, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House
