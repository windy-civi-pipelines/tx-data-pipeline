89(2) HR 44 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 44 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Family and friends will forever cherish their memories of Charlene Cravotta Harber of Dallas, who was tragically lost in the July 2025 flooding in Kerr County along with her husband, William Michael "Mike" Harber, and her granddaughters, Blair and Brooke Harber; and WHEREAS, The former Charlene Cravotta was born in 1950 to Charles and Mae Cravotta, and she grew up in a family that also included her four siblings, Lisa, Jimmy, Brenda, and Chuck; and WHEREAS, A caring wife, mother, and grandmother, Mrs. Harber shared a fulfilling relationship with her husband, and she took great pride in her children, RJ and Jennifer, and in her two granddaughters; and WHEREAS, Mrs. Harber benefited many students through her service as a tutor at several private schools in the Dallas area, including The Episcopal School of Dallas and Parish Episcopal School, and she and Mr. Harber were known for their friendliness and zest for life; and WHEREAS, Although she will be deeply missed, the passage of time will never diminish the joy and love that Charlene Harber brought into the lives of all who knew her; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Charlene Cravotta Harber and extend sincere condolences to the members of her family: to her son, RJ Harber, and his wife, Annie; to her daughter, Jennifer Harber; to her sister, Lisa Cravotta King; to her brother, Jimmy Cravotta; and to her other relatives and many friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Charlene Harber. Meyer ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 44 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on August 27, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House
