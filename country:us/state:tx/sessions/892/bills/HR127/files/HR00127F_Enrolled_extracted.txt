89(2) HR 127 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 127 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, The passing of Lainey Anne Landry of Houston, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County, at the age of nine, has brought an unimaginable loss to the many people who loved and cherished her; and WHEREAS, Born on March 30, 2016, Lainey Landry was the daughter of devoted parents Natalie and Ben Landry, and her treasured family included her two brothers, Daniel and Luke, as well as her grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins; a kind-hearted and spirited young girl, she loved baking muffins with her grandmother, bonding with her grandparents over card games, and caring for the family pets, Lila, Yawyaw, and Doc; she brightened the days of everyone around her with her beautiful smile, her sense of humor, and her gift for making others feel special; and WHEREAS, A student at St. Michael Catholic School, Lainey showed off her talents on the school's basketball court and also enjoyed playing soccer and participating in Girl Scouts; she expressed her growing faith as a member of St. Theresa Catholic Church alongside her family, and her receipt of her first Holy Communion in May marked a significant step in her spiritual journey; and WHEREAS, Lainey Landry was a ray of light in the lives of all those who held her dear, and although her passing has brought tremendous sorrow, her legacy of love will never be forgotten; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Lainey Anne Landry and extend heartfelt condolences to the members of her family: to her parents, Natalie and Ben Landry; to her brothers, Daniel Timothy and Luke Joseph Landry; to her grandparents, Brenda Landry and Susan and Timothy Weaver; to her aunts and uncles, Stephanie and Will Landry, Katherine and David Andrew, and Jamie and Laura Weaver; to her cousins, Graham William and Hallie Lynn Landry, Cullen Lee, Sophia Elaine, and Wesley Gray Andrew, and Wynne Ruth Weaver; and to her other relatives and dear friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Lainey Anne Landry. Hull ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 127 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 3, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House
