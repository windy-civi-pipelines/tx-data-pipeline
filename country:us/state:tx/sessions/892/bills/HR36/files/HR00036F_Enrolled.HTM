Title: 89(2) HR 36 - Enrolled version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Enrolled Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 36 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 36 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the profound sense of sorrow felt by the family and friends of Eloise Elizabeth Peck of Dallas, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County; and WHEREAS, Affectionately known by her nickname "Lulu," Eloise Peck was born on September 18, 2016, and she was eight years old at the time of her passing; the daughter of Missy and Tim Peck, she was blessed with the companionship of her younger brother, Holden, and she benefited from the love and support of an extended family that included her grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, and godparents, along with her Boston terrier, Ollie, who was seldom far from her side; and WHEREAS, Eloise was a student at Bradfield Elementary School, and her favorite part of the day was recess, when she often guided her classmates in their work at "Lulu's Spa"; outside of school hours, she enjoyed playing with Holden, visiting her family's Flying R Ranch, and exploring nature on her bicycle; she often regaled her father with fanciful stories of her outdoor adventures, and she spent many happy hours talking and staging dance parties with her mother; she demonstrated her kindness through her efforts to make others feel connected and included, and she had a special place in her heart for every animal she encountered; and WHEREAS, Although her time on this earth was far too brief, Eloise Peck was a ray of light in the lives of those who held her dear, and they will never forget the joy, laughter, and love that she shared with them; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Eloise Elizabeth Peck and extend heartfelt condolences to the members of her family: to her parents, Missy and Tim Peck; to her brother, Holden Peck; to her grandparents, Beth "Cha Cha" Rogers and Rob Rogers and Jean "Nini" Wolitarsky and Jim Wolitarsky; to her aunts and uncles, Becca and Chandler Quisenberry, Joan and Michael O'Connor, Kim and Jamie Wolitarsky, and Matt Wolitarsky; to her cousins, Dottie and James Quisenberry, Ellie and TJ O'Connor, and Brooke and William Wolitarsky; to her godparents, Neal Thomas and Phillips Nazro; and to her other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Lulu Peck. Meyer ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 36 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on August 27, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 36 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 36 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the profound sense of sorrow felt by the family and friends of Eloise Elizabeth Peck of Dallas, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County;
