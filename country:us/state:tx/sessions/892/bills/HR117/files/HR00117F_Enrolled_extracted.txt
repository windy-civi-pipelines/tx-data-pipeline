89(2) HR 117 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 117 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, The family and many friends of Katherine Frances Ferruzzo of Houston will forever cherish their memories of this vibrant and beloved young woman, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County at the age of 19; and WHEREAS, Born on June 21, 2006, to John and Andrea DeLorenzo Ferruzzo, Katherine Ferruzzo grew up with the companionship of two older brothers, Jack and Vincent; during her time at Memorial High School in Houston, she excelled academically and in several leadership roles, serving as a student council representative for four years and as chapter president of the Texas Association of Future Educators; she also distinguished herself through her membership in both the National Honor Society and the English National Honor Society; her dynamic personality earned her the title of "Life of the Party" by her Class of 2025 peers, and she carried that same contagious energy into her role as head of the senior girls' spirit organization; and WHEREAS, Ms. Ferruzzo's acceptance to The University of Texas at Austin was a joyous achievement for her and a proud moment for her parents, both of whom are alumni; inspired by her childhood experience with speech therapy, she planned to pursue a degree in special education, hoping to positively impact the lives of children facing unique learning challenges; she had gained experience in the field through her involvement with the Spring Branch Independent School District APPLE program for students with autism, and she also volunteered countless hours with the Memorial Chapter of the National Charity League alongside her mother; in recognition of her outstanding efforts, she was honored with the President's Volunteer Service Award three years in a row; and WHEREAS, Ms. Ferruzzo attended Camp Mystic in Hunt from 2015 to 2024, and she was profoundly influenced by her experiences and achievements there, especially her election as a tribe captain in her final year as a camper; she was excited to return as a counselor in 2025, and she spent her final days there, caring for the campers of the Bubble Inn cabin; and WHEREAS, Katherine Ferruzzo accomplished much and touched the lives of many people during her brief time on this earth, and her memory will continue to inspire those who were blessed to have known her; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life of Katherine Frances Ferruzzo and extend sincere condolences to all who mourn her passing; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Katherine Ferruzzo. DeAyala ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 117 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 2, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House
