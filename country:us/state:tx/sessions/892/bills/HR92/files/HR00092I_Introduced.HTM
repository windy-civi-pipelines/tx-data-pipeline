Title: 89(2) HR 92 - Introduced version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Introduced Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 92 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21010 BPG-D By: Martinez Fischer H.R. No. 92 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A life filled with joyful times and meaningful accomplishments drew to a close with the passing of Thomas Irving Hennigan of San Antonio on August 1, 2025, at the age of 83; and WHEREAS, The son of Marguerite Thomas Belisle and Ray Hennigan, Tom Hennigan was born in Alexandria, Louisiana, on August 21, 1941; his entrepreneurial spirit led him to a career in the real estate industry in partnership with his wife, Peggy Koesterman Hennigan; he opened a loan servicing company and became involved in investor education; as president of the New Orleans Real Estate Investor Group, he expanded membership from 35 to more than 800; traveling the country for seminars and conferences, he met with his counterparts and recognized the potential of a nationwide network; his organization hosted a meeting in New Orleans in 1985, and its success led the Hennigans to establish the conference that became the National Real Estate Investors Association; he served as president of the organization, which today has over 30,000 members; he also founded the San Antonio REIA; while pursuing a successful career as an investor, landlord, and rehabber, he was always happy to mentor others and share his encyclopedic knowledge of real estate; and WHEREAS, Tom and Peggy Hennigan were the proud parents of a son, Stephen, and their treasured family grew to include two granddaughters, Megan and Callie; the devoted couple shared a fulfilling 46-year relationship until Mrs. Hennigan's passing in 2010; fortunate to find love a second time, Mr. Hennigan married the former Lucha Esquivel on February 4, 2013; among his favorite pastimes were visiting his beloved New Orleans, traveling the world, and cruising the Caribbean with his friends and the National REIA groups; outgoing and optimistic, he brightened the days of all he encountered with his warmth, kindness, and dry sense of humor; and WHEREAS, While his loss is difficult to bear, Tom Hennigan leaves behind numerous friends and loved ones whose lives are far richer for having known him, and he will forever hold a special place in their hearts; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Thomas Irving Hennigan and extend sincere sympathy to the members of his family: to his wife, Lucha Esquivel Hennigan; to his son, Stephen Hennigan, and his wife, Tracy; to his grandchildren, Megan and Callie Hennigan; to his sister, Judy Klopp; to his nephews, Joe, Jimmy, and Nicholas Impastato; to his stepchildren, Celeste Goshorn and Eric Mayworm; and to his many other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Tom Hennigan. ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 92 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21010 BPG-D By: Martinez Fischer H.R. No. 92 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A life filled with joyful times and meaningful accomplishments drew to a close with the passing of Thomas Irving Hennigan of San Antonio on August 1, 2025, at the age of 83;
