89(2) HR 115 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21164 KRT-D By: DeAyala H.R. No. 115 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the sorrow felt at the passing of nine-year-old Molly Claire DeWitt of Houston, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County; and WHEREAS, The daughter of Ryan and Elizabeth DeWitt, Molly DeWitt was born in Houston on June 10, 2016, and she was blessed with the companionship of a sister, Annie; her circle of love and support also included her grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins; and WHEREAS, Molly was a creative young girl who delighted in drawing, dancing, and telling stories; insatiably curious, she was a voracious reader, and she was always eager to learn more about the world; she also enjoyed playing soccer with her friends; and WHEREAS, Known for her compassion, Molly was quick to extend kindness to those in need; she was also confident and unafraid to speak her mind, and her vivid blue-green eyes helped her to connect with those around her and to make them feel seen; her faith played a guiding role in her life, shaping her to love with sincerity, generosity, and grace beyond her years; and WHEREAS, Although her days were far too few, the joy and radiance with which Molly DeWitt lived her life will forever comfort and inspire all who were fortunate enough to know her; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Molly Claire DeWitt and extend deepest condolences to the members of her family:  to her parents, Ryan and Elizabeth DeWitt; to her sister, Annie DeWitt; to her grandparents, Chris and Mary Anne DeWitt and Max and Vicki Garrett; to her aunts and uncles, Sean and Trudy DeWitt, Brendan and Virginia DeWitt, Kevin and Natalie DeWitt, Tyler and Abby Garrett, and Charlotte Garrett; to her cousins, Reagan, Riley, Adelaide, McAllen, Henry, and Conor DeWitt and Audrey and Davis Garrett; and to her other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Molly DeWitt.
