Title: 89(2) HR 119 - Enrolled version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Enrolled Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 119 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 119 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the sorrow felt at the passing of eight-year-old Sarah Catherine Marsh of Mountain Brook, Alabama, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County; and WHEREAS, The daughter of Jill and Patrick Marsh, Sarah Marsh was born on May 30, 2017; she grew up surrounded by the love of her extended family along with many self-appointed "aunts" and "uncles" in Birmingham, Alabama, as well as in Dallas, Waco, and Arlington, but most of all, she adored her big brother, John; although she sometimes teased him as "annoying," they were the best of friends, teaming up for creative mischief, snuggling on the couch to watch slime videos, and hugging each other when they crossed paths in the school hallways; and WHEREAS, Sarah loved tagging along to meet her mother's patients, and she was an attentive "surgical assistant" for such procedures as the stitching up of her father's finger after another kitchen mishap; in addition, she was enthralled by her father's students at Samford University; and WHEREAS, A joyful and adventurous little girl, Sarah demonstrated a streak of sass so charming that one of her teachers confessed her reluctance to repress it; her vibrant personality and radiant smile brought her many friends, and she was fiercely loyal to each of them; she enjoyed showing off her splits or a new dance she had learned with her pals; and WHEREAS, Although it is immeasurably painful to say goodbye to one so young and full of promise, all who shared in the life of Sarah Marsh will treasure their memories of each happy moment spent in the company of this vibrant, kind, and engaging child; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life of Sarah Catherine Marsh and extend sincere condolences to the members of her family: to her parents, Jill Waters Marsh and Patrick Marsh; to her brother, John Marsh; to her grandparents, Jenger and Steve Waters and Debbie and Barclay Marsh; to her aunts, uncles, and cousins, David, Robin, Brooke, and Foster Waters and Jordan, Allison, Logan, and Cameron Marsh; and to her many other relatives and host of friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Sarah Marsh. DeAyala ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 119 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 2, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 119 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 119 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Words cannot adequately express the sorrow felt at the passing of eight-year-old Sarah Catherine Marsh of Mountain Brook, Alabama, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County;
