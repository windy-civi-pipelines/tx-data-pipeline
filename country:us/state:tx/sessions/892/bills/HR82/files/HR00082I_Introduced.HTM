R E S O L U T I O N
         WHEREAS, The U.S. Army marked the 250th anniversary of the
  Continental Army on June 14, 2025, in Houston, Texas; and
         WHEREAS, On June 14, 1775, the delegates to the Second
  Continental Congress established the Continental Army as a means
  for the 13 unified American colonies to fight Britain; the soldiers
  in the six newly created companies of expert riflemen took an oath
  of enlistment, and on June 15, Congress elected George Washington
  as commander in chief of the colonies' first official army; its
  battle cry was "This We'll Defend," a motto that endures today;
  after the Declaration of Independence was adopted on July 4, 1776,
  the Continental Army and the militia serving Congress became known
  as the Army of the United States; and
         WHEREAS, The modern army is composed of active-duty troops as
  well as two reserve components, the Army Reserve and the Army
  National Guard; the operational army conducts missions around the
  world through the service of its numbered armies, corps, divisions,
  brigades, and battalions, and the institutional army provides the
  infrastructure and support necessary to train, equip, deploy, and
  ensure the readiness of all army forces; and
         WHEREAS, For two and a half centuries, the U.S. Army has
  played a critical role in the development and defense of the nation
  while safeguarding its values and interests, and this momentous
  milestone provides a welcome opportunity to acknowledge the immense
  debt of gratitude we owe to the men and women who have carried out
  its vital mission; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas
  Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby commemorate the 250th
  anniversary of the U.S. Army; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be
  prepared for the U.S. Army's 5th Brigade as an expression of high
  regard by the Texas House of Representatives.
