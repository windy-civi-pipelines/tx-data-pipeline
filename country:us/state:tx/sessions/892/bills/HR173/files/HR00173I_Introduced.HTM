Title: 89(2) HR 173 - Introduced version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Introduced Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 173 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21373 KRT-D By: Leach H.R. No. 173 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A life made rich through meaningful service drew to a close with the passing of Jennifer Lynn Young Cook of Plano on August 18, 2025, at the age of 38; and WHEREAS, The daughter of Gary and Lisa Young, the former Jennifer Young was born in Wylie on September 1, 1986; after she graduated from high school, she earned her associate's degree in child development from Collin College; and WHEREAS, Over the course of a rewarding career in education, Mrs. Cook worked as a day-care teacher in church programs, and she served the Weekday Program at First United Methodist Church Plano for more than a decade; admired for her warmth and patience, she positively impacted the lives of hundreds of young children and their families; a woman of deep faith, her work as a caregiver reflected her Christian values; and WHEREAS, Above all else, Mrs. Cook was a devoted wife to her husband, Jason, and a loving mother to her children, Dylan and Lena; even in the final months of her battle with cancer, she remained determined to create lasting memories with her family; and WHEREAS, Those fortunate enough to have known Jennifer Cook will remember the way she touched their lives with her infectious laugh, her resilience, and her eternal optimism, and she will forever hold a treasured place in their hearts; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life of Jennifer Lynn Young Cook and extend deepest condolences to the members of her family: to her husband, Jason Cook; to her son, Dylan Cook; to her daughter, Lena Cook; to her mother, Lisa Young; to her nephew, Aiden; to her grandparents, Joann and Jerry Huff; to her in-laws, Bob and Linda Cook; and to her other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Jennifer Cook. ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 173 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21373 KRT-D By: Leach H.R. No. 173 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A life made rich through meaningful service drew to a close with the passing of Jennifer Lynn Young Cook of Plano on August 18, 2025, at the age of 38;
