89(2) HR 116 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21152 CW-D By: DeAyala H.R. No. 116 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Family and friends are mourning the passing of eight-year-old Lucy Dillon of Houston, who was tragically lost in the July 4, 2025, flooding in Kerr County; and WHEREAS, Born to Brandt and Lisa Dillon on December 17, 2016, in Houston, Lucy Dillon was a source of tremendous joy to her parents; she treasured the time she spent with her sister, Charley, and her half-sisters, Maren and Miranda, and her circle of love and support also included her grandparents, Suki and Joe Posevina; and WHEREAS, A rising third grader at Memorial Drive Elementary School, Lucy was a proud "math mover" who was known for her academic success, her insatiable curiosity, and her remarkable memory; moreover, her love of learning extended to fun facts and sports statistics; she was an avid reader, and she collected crystals, gems, bobblehead athletes, fidget toys, and anything featuring corgis; and WHEREAS, Lucy made friends everywhere she went, and she was a gifted athlete who began riding a bicycle when she was three years old and playing basketball at age four; a devoted Houston Rockets fan, she delighted in shooting baskets in the driveway with the members of her family; fiercely competitive, she had recently helped her basketball and softball teams win league championships, and she dreamed of becoming a college basketball player; and WHEREAS, Although her time on this earth was far too brief, Lucy Dillon was a ray of light in the lives of those who held her dear, and they will never forget the kindness, passion, and determination that she brought to her every endeavor; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Lucy Dillon and extend sincere condolences to the members of her family: to her parents, Brandt and Lisa Dillon; to her sister, Charley Dillon; to her half-sisters, Maren Dillon and Miranda Dillon Hassler; to her maternal grandparents, Suki Posevina and Joe Posevina; to her uncle, Michael Posevina; and to all those who share in the sorrow of her loss; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for her family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Lucy Dillon.
