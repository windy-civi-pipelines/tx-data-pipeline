89(2) HR 132 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21167 DSH-D By: Guillen H.R. No. 132 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A rich and purposeful life drew to a close with the passing of Luis V. Allala Jr. of Cotulla on July 12, 2025, at the age of 88; and WHEREAS, The son of Luis Ayala Sr. and Florencia Valle Ayala, Luis Allala was born in Cotulla on February 10, 1937, and his family included his five siblings, Amador, Jose, Gustavo, Jesus, and Eva, and his four half-siblings, Maria, Luis Martin, Alfredo, and Isaac; and WHEREAS, After attending Cotulla High School, Mr. Allala moved to Des Plaines, Illinois, where he started a successful multigenerational irrigation business; in the course of his entrepreneurial career, he was also involved in real estate investment, restaurant ownership, and farming and ranching; he was especially passionate about building a connection to the land he grew up on, and he took great pride in establishing the Double LL Ranch; moreover, he was active in his community until well into his later years, and he was elected alderman at the age of 82; and WHEREAS, Mr. Allala enjoyed the love and support of his wife, Yolanda, with whom he shared a rewarding marriage that spanned more than six and a half decades before her passing; together, the couple raised three children, Luis, Linda, and Leroy, and over the years, they welcomed six grandchildren and three great-grandchildren into their family; in his leisure hours, Mr. Allala took pleasure in gardening, listening to mariachi music, and watching old Western movies, but above all, he cherished time spent with his loved ones; and WHEREAS, Generous, intelligent, and kind, Luis Allala brightened the world for innumerable people, and they will forever hold him close in their hearts; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life of Luis V. Allala Jr. and extend sincere condolences to the members of his family: to his children, Luis Allala III, Linda Rossi, and Leroy Allala; to his grandchildren, Lylianna, Luis, Lucinda, Ricardo, Gabriella, and Easton; to his great-grandchildren, Vida, Isla, and Luna; and to his other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Luis Allala.
