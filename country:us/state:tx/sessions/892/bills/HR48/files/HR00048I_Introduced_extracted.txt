89(2) HR 48 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S20416 KRT-D By: Lujan H.R. No. 48 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, July 5, 2025, marked the 10th anniversary of the designation of the San Antonio Missions as a UNESCO World Heritage Site; and WHEREAS, Through its World Heritage program, UNESCO recognizes important cultural sites around the globe and encourages their preservation; since receiving the designation in 2015, the collection of five historic missions in San Antonio has had the distinction of being the first and only UNESCO World Heritage Site in Texas as well as one of only 24 in the entire United States; and WHEREAS, The San Antonio Missions site includes Mission San Antonio de Valero, or the Alamo, Mission Concepción, Mission San José, Mission San Juan, and Mission Espada; situated along a stretch of the San Antonio River basin, these frontier mission complexes encompass churches and other historic structures, including residencies and granaries, as well as a ranch southeast of San Antonio; and WHEREAS, The complexes trace their history to the early 18th century, when Franciscan missionaries led the effort to colonize, evangelize, and defend the northern frontier of New Spain; the missions included all the components required to establish self-sustaining communities in what was then a remote part of Spain's North American empire; in addition, the missions illustrate the interweaving of the Spanish colonial and Indigenous cultures, as illustrated by features such as the decorative elements of churches, which combine Catholic symbols with nature-inspired designs derived from the Native Americans who settled at the complexes; and WHEREAS, The San Antonio Missions reflect the rich cultural heritage of the Lone Star State, and it is a pleasure to join in honoring these historic landmarks on this special occasion; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby commemorate the 10th anniversary of the designation of the San Antonio Missions as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and extend to all those affiliated with the site sincere best wishes for the future; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for the missions as an expression of high regard by the Texas House of Representatives.
