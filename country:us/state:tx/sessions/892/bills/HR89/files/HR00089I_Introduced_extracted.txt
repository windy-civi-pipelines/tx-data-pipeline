89(2) HR 89 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21077 KSM-D By: Harris Davila H.R. No. 89 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, The 90th birthday of an esteemed Texan is indeed cause for celebration, and Ronald Paul Werner of Taylor marked that special milestone on June 26, 2025; and WHEREAS, Ron Werner was born in Memphis, Tennessee, in 1935; his father passed away when he was a young child, and he and his two siblings were raised by their loving mother, Ruth; he earned a degree in industrial engineering from Memphis State University in 1958; answering his nation's call to duty, he joined the U.S. Army and served as a combat engineer and missile specialist at Fort Bliss; after concluding his enlistment, he remained in El Paso, where he met the love of his life, the former Pat Ray, during a group picnic at Hueco Tanks; the couple were engaged a week later and married on December 27, 1961; over the years, they have been blessed with two children, Janet and Stanley, and three grandchildren, Axel, Peyton, and Bradley; and WHEREAS, During the early 1960s, Mr. Werner worked as a designer at the Kerr-Ban Furniture plant in Taylor; he later decided to attend dental school, and he and his family lived with his mother for five years while he completed his studies; in 1972, they returned to Taylor, where he established his own dental practice; and WHEREAS, Active in his community, Mr. Werner has been involved in numerous civic organizations, including parent-teacher associations, study clubs, and church groups; he is also a longtime supporter of the Republican Party and has served as a precinct chair and poll watcher; in addition, he was a member of the Taylor Independent School District board of trustees for 18 years; his honors include recognition as Citizen of the Year by the local Rotary Club in 1983; and WHEREAS, Ron Werner has witnessed a panorama of change over the course of nine decades, and he has met life's challenges and opportunities with dignity and grace, earning the deep admiration of all who are privileged to know him; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby congratulate Ronald Paul Werner on the occasion of his 90th birthday and extend to him sincere best wishes for continued happiness; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for Mr. Werner as an expression of high regard by the Texas House of Representatives.
