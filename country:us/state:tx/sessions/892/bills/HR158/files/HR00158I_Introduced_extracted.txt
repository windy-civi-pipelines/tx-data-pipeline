89(2) HR 158 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S21319 CJM-D By: Dyson H.R. No. 158 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Scotty's House Brazos Valley Child Advocacy Center in Bryan is celebrating its 30th anniversary in 2025; and WHEREAS, Founded in 1995, Scotty's House honors the memory of Timothy Scott Linerode, a four-year-old boy who was tragically shot to death by his father after being abducted from a day-care center; committed to assisting children who have suffered abuse and neglect, the nonprofit provides a supportive and nurturing environment for youth referred to its care by law enforcement and Child Protective Services; and WHEREAS, The organization's dedicated staff works with CPS personnel, law enforcement, and other officials at all stages of the investigative process to help children achieve safety, healing, and justice; services provided at Scotty's House include counseling and advocacy, as well as forensic interviews and medical exams; moreover, the organization strives to strengthen families and to unite the community against child abuse; since opening its doors, Scotty's House has served more than 14,000 children from Brazos, Burleson, Grimes, Leon, Madison, Robertson, and Washington Counties; and WHEREAS, Over the past three decades, Scotty's House has made an immeasurable difference in the lives of some of the most vulnerable residents of the Brazos Valley, and this noteworthy milestone offers a fitting opportunity to recognize the organization for its commendable work; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby commemorate the 30th anniversary of Scotty's House Brazos Valley Child Advocacy Center and extend to all those associated with the organization sincere best wishes for the future; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for Scotty's House as an expression of high regard by the Texas House of Representatives.
