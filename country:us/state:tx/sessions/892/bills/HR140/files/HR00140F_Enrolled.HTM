Title: 89(2) HR 140 - Enrolled version - Bill Text Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Enrolled Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: 89(2) HR 140 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 140 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Memories of a life well lived may help to comfort those saddened by the death of Roldan Montalvo Jr. of Hebbronville on July 12, 2025, at the age of 80; and WHEREAS, The son of Roldan Montalvo Sr. and Cristela Almaraz Montalvo, Roldan Montalvo was born on February 12, 1945, in Alice and grew up sharing in the companionship of two siblings, Dalia and David; after serving as a state game warden, he embarked on a rewarding career as an educator that included tenures as a golf coach, a high school assistant principal, and a teacher at Hebbronville Elementary School; he also worked a second job at the family business, Roldan's Texaco; and WHEREAS, Valuing family above all else, Mr. Montalvo was a devoted husband to his wife, Anna, and he took great pride in his children, Melissa, Jessica, and Roldan; with the passing years, he delighted in becoming a grandfather to eight grandchildren, Eric, Ricardo, Austin, Dalyssa, Kayden, Seth, Omar, and Karina, and he was further blessed with five great-grandchildren, Abran, Ameli, Mia, Gianna, and Graeson; in his leisure hours, he enjoyed playing golf and watching his favorite sports teams, the Dallas Cowboys and the Houston Astros; and WHEREAS, Those who were privileged to share in this esteemed man's love and friendship will always remember him with great admiration and affection; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Roldan Montalvo Jr. and extend sincere condolences to the members of his family:  to his wife, Anna R. Montalvo; to his children, Melissa Dale, Jessica Curbow and her husband, Sean, and Roldan Javier Montalvo and his wife, Jessica; to his grandchildren, Eric Montalvo, Ricardo Ramirez, Austin Dale, Dalyssa G. Perez, Kayden Curbow, Seth Curbow, Omar Montalvo, and Karina Montalvo; to his great-grandchildren, Abran Ramirez, Ameli Montalvo, Mia Montalvo, Gianna Montalvo, and Graeson Perez; to his siblings, Dalia Johnson and David Montalvo and his wife, Gilda; and to his other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas House of Representatives adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Roldan Montalvo. Guillen ______________________________ Speaker of the House I certify that H.R. No. 140 was unanimously adopted by a rising vote of the House on September 3, 2025. ______________________________ Chief Clerk of the House ================================================================================ Raw Text: 89(2) HR 140 - Enrolled version - Bill Text H.R. No. 140 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, Memories of a life well lived may help to comfort those saddened by the death of Roldan Montalvo Jr. of Hebbronville on July 12, 2025, at the age of 80; and WHEREAS, The son of Roldan Montalvo Sr. and Cristela Almaraz Montalvo, Roldan Montalvo was born on February 12, 1945, in Alice and grew up sharing in the companionship of two siblings, Dalia and David; after serving as a state game warden, he embarked on a rewarding career as an educator that included tenures as a golf coach, a high school assistant principal, and a teacher at Hebbronville Elementary School; he also worked a second job at the family business, Roldan's Texaco; and WHEREAS, Valuing family above all else, Mr. Montalvo was a devoted husband to his wife, Anna, and he took great pride in his children, Melissa, Jessica, and Roldan; with the passing years, he delighted in becoming a grandfather to eight grandchildren, Eric, Ricardo, Austin, Dalyssa, Kayden, Seth, Omar, and Karina, and he was further blessed with five great-grandchildren, Abran, Ameli, Mia, Gianna, and Graeson; in his leisure hours, he enjoyed playing golf and watching his favorite sports teams, the Dallas Cowboys and the Houston Astros; and WHEREAS, Those who were privileged to share in this esteemed man's love and friendship will always remember him with great admiration and affection; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature, 2nd Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Roldan Montalvo Jr. and extend sincere condolences to the members of his family:  to his wife, Anna R.
